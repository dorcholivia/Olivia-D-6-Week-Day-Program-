Health Resources

Physical Health

Understanding your body's functionality is key to maintaining good health. Regular exercise, balanced nutrition, and adequate sleep form the foundation of physical wellbeing.

Common Questions About Physical Health:

  • Q: How much exercise do I need daily?
    A: Most health authorities recommend at least 30 minutes of moderate activity 5 days a week, or 150 minutes total weekly.
  • Q: What are signs I should see a doctor?
    A: Persistent pain, sudden weight changes, unusual fatigue, changes in sleep patterns, or any symptoms that significantly impact your daily life warrant medical attention.
  • Q: How can I improve my cardiovascular health?
    A: Regular aerobic exercise, maintaining healthy blood pressure and cholesterol levels, not smoking, and eating a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

Mental Health

Mental health is equally important as physical health. Taking care of your mind involves managing stress, practicing mindfulness, and seeking support when needed.

Common Questions About Mental Health:

  • Q: How can I manage daily stress?
    A: Practice deep breathing exercises, maintain a regular sleep schedule, engage in physical activity, limit screen time, and consider mindfulness meditation.
  • Q: What are signs of anxiety I should watch for?
    A: Excessive worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, and sleep problems are common signs of anxiety.
  • Q: When should I seek professional help for mental health?
    A: If feelings of sadness, anxiety, or other mental health concerns persist for weeks and interfere with daily activities, relationships, or work, it's time to consult a professional.

Health Risk Assessment

Understanding potential health risks can help you make informed decisions about your lifestyle and preventive care.

Common Questions About Health Risks:

  • Q: What lifestyle factors increase health risks?
    A: Smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, poor diet, physical inactivity, high stress levels, and inadequate sleep all contribute to increased health risks.
  • Q: How does family history affect my health risks?
    A: Family history can indicate genetic predispositions to certain conditions like heart disease, diabetes, and some cancers. Sharing this information with your healthcare provider helps create appropriate screening schedules.
  • Q: What preventive screenings should I consider?
    A: Recommended screenings vary by age, gender, and risk factors. Common ones include blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes, various cancers, and mental health assessments.
